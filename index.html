about

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Yale, advised by Dr. David van Dijk. My research develops machine-learning methods for biological data, including representing single-cell gene expression data as natural language (Cell2Sentence), modeling cell-cell interactions in spatial transcriptomics with ligand-receptor priors, and vision-language models and agentic systems for interpreting and analyzing fMRI recordings. I am interested in interpretable and multimodal models, few-shot evaluation, and workflows that integrate prior biological knowledge. Before graduate school I worked as a software engineer and did a dual Masters at the University of Michigan in Computer Science and Mathematics. Outside the lab you can probably find me in a coffee shop or bookstore, hiking a trail, or enjoying a craft project.
